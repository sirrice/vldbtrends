Thanks to @samrmadden for the 2000 - 2011 titles.

What's hot?

Here, we look for keywords whose max(count)/min(count) is maximized, and where the peak happens after the trough.

With several best papers in the past years, the explosion of bigdata networks, and many new research and industrial systems, Graphs are consistently on the rise. Mapreduce and cloud had their time and are now giving way to more specialized "big data" systems. Uncertainty popped up as a topic several years back. Exploratory analysis like matching, searching, and similarity have always been around, but are getting more popular. Did we just discover that fast queries are better queries? Probably not, we just emphasize it explicitly now.

The New Hotness

Let's now look at keywords that have burst into the scene in the past few years. Maybe you want to work on these problems. These keywords are selected by computing the ratio of "the average number of times a keyword is used since 2010" by "the average before 2010".

New hardware like flash, and gpus are taking over. Could NVRam soon join the club? Scaling bigdata is still at the peak of the gartner hype cycle, and there are so many systems that it's hard to even compare them.

Finally it's nice to see that crowdsourcing is still trending upwards. Perhaps work with a more human and social angle is ready for the lime light.researchers always love hybrid solutions.
